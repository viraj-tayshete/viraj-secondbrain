## video syntax 


3. Problem – cluttered status quo (0–4 s)
    
    > “Show a cluttered, abstract digital workspace representing the current problem (e.g., messy dashboards, too many tools, confusing data). Multiple dull UI windows and icons float around in a slightly chaotic way. Camera slowly pushes in. Colors are cooler or desaturated to imply friction. On-screen caption: ‘[Problem line, e.g., Managing data across tools is chaos.]’”
    
4. Problem emphasis – frustration (4–7 s)
    
    > “Cut between 2–3 closer shots of unhelpful or ‘stuck’ UI states (generic charts not improving, notifications piling up, etc., no real text). Small camera parallax in each shot. Elements jitter slightly or move in unproductive loops. Caption: a short problem phrase that builds tension.”
    
5. Solution reveal – product appears (7–10 s)
    
    > “Cut to a clean, open 3D space. The new SaaS product logo or name assembles from simple 3D shapes that fly in and snap together. Camera does a slow orbit around the logo. Background gradient shifts to brighter brand colors. Caption: ‘Meet [Product Name].’ Tone switches from tense to confi
    > dent.”
    
6. Product UI hero – main dashboard (10–16 s)
    
    > “Reveal a 3D-stylized version of the desktop SaaS dashboard, floating at a slight angle. Panels slide in from the side with soft easing and a tiny overshoot. Icons pop in with a small scale bounce. Camera makes a slow left-to-right move with parallax. Caption: one clear benefit line, e.g., ‘One place for all your [X].’”
    
7. Feature highlights – 2–3 beats (16–24 s)
    
    > “Show 2–3 quick feature shots. Each shot focuses on one key feature card filling most of the frame. The card animates in (slide/scale), one simple interaction or data animation plays, then cut to the next. Average 1–1.2 seconds per feature. Camera mostly static per card with subtle push-in. Captions: short feature lines like ‘Automated reports’, ‘Smart suggestions’, ‘Real-time alerts’.”
    
8. Outcome / transformation – system working (24–30 s)
    
    > “Pull back to a wider shot of a clean, organized system: data or tasks flowing smoothly through stages into the new SaaS. Icons or data blobs move along clear paths and change color/size to indicate success or progress. Camera slowly dollies out or tilts to reveal the whole flow. Colors are bright and harmonious. Caption: ‘Turn chaos into clear, actionable insight.’ (Replace with your outcome message.)”
    
9. CTA – final frame (30–35 s)
    
    > “End on a minimal closing frame: centered logo, short tagline, and a CTA text bar resembling a button or URL. Background is clean with only a few subtle floating shapes or a soft glow. Camera is static or has a very slight push-in. Hold this shot for at least 2–3 seconds. CTA example text: ‘Start your free trial at [domain]’ or ‘Book a demo today.’”

Motion, rhythm, and emotion

> “Animate everything with smooth easing, minimal overshoot, and no jitter. Use clean slides, curves, and scale-ins. Align most cuts with music beats and/or voice-over phrases, keeping the early problem section slightly quicker and the solution/CTA sections more relaxed. Start with a cooler, slightly cluttered look for the problem, then gradually transition to brighter, more open compositions as the product solves the issue.”[](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgmuplXU2iY)​

- Average cuts: 1–2 seconds; slightly faster in the problem, slightly slower in product reveal + CTA.[](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgmuplXU2iY)​
    
- Emotional arc: Tense → confident → optimistic.
